### 1. Find $V_{Th}$ (Open-Circuit Voltage)
*   **Remove the load resistance $R_L$** between terminals $a$ and $b$.
*   **Identify the controlling variable:**
    
    *   Locate the variable that controls the dependent source (e.g., $v_1$).
        
    *   Express it in terms of the node voltage at terminal $a$ ($V_a$) and the source voltage.
        
    *   _Example:_ $v_1 = 12 - V_a$.
        
*   **Apply Nodal Analysis (KCL):**
    
    *   Sum the currents leaving node $a$ and set them to zero.
        
    *   Example: $\frac{V_a - 12}{4} - 4 + \frac{V_a + 2v_1}{2 + 4} = 0$.
        
*   **Solve for $V_a$**

### 2. Find $I_{sc}$ (Short-Circuit Current)

*   **Place short circuit**  between terminals $a$ and $b$.
        
*   **Calculate $I_{sc}$ using KCL:**
    
    *   Sum the currents leaving node $a$, including the current $I_{sc}$ flowing through the short.
        
    *   _Example:_ $I_{sc} + \frac{0 - 12}{4} - 4 + \frac{0 + 2(12)}{6} = 0 \Rightarrow I_{sc} = 3 \text{ A}$.
        
### 3. Calculate $R_{Th}$ and Max Power

*   **Find Thévenin Resistance:** Use the ratio $R_{Th} = \frac{V_{Th}}{I_{sc}}$.

        
*   **Maximum Power Condition:** Set $R_L = R_{Th}$.
    
*   **Calculate $P_{max}$:** Use the formula $P_{max} = \frac{V_{Th}^2}{4R_{Th}}$.
    
    *   _Example:_ $P_{max} = \frac{36^2}{4(12)} = 27 \text{ W}$.

*   **Step 2: Apply Continuity ($t = 0^+$):**

<div style="background-color: #e8f4fd; padding: 5px; border-left: 4px solid #2196F3; border-radius: 4px;">

<p>Due to the <strong>continuity of capacitor voltage and inductor current</strong> </p>

$$ 
\begin{aligned} i_L(0^+) &= i_L(0^-) \\ v_C(0^+) &= v_C(0^-) \end{aligned} 
$$
</div>  


*   **Step 3: Find Final State ($t \rightarrow \infty$):**
    
    *   Redraw circuit for the new switch position and find steady-state values ($i_L(\infty)$ or $v_C(\infty)$).
        

*   **Step 4: Find Time Constant ($\tau$):**
    
       **RL:** $\tau = \frac{L}{R_{eq}}$.
        
       **RC:** $\tau = R_{eq}C$.
        

   _$R_{eq}$ is the resistance seen by the L or C after the switch moves._
        
*   **Step 5: Master Equation:**

$$x(t) = x(\infty) + [x(0^+) - x(\infty)]e^{-t/\tau}$$

Where $x(t)$ can be either $i(t)$ or $v(t)$.

RLC circuits to find values and derivatives immediately after switching.

*   **Initial Values:** Use the continuity rules ($v_C(0^+)$ and $i_L(0^+)$) first-order.
    
*   **Voltage Derivative (Capacitor):**
    
    *   Identify current $i_C(0^+)$ entering the positive terminal.
        
    *   **Formula:** $\frac{dv_C(0^+)}{dt} = \frac{i_C(0^+)}{C}$.
        
*   **Current Derivative (Inductor):**
    
    *   Identify voltage $v_L(0^+)$ across the inductor.
        
    *   Apply KVL around the active loop at $t = 0^+$.
        
    *   **Formula:** $\frac{di_L(0^+)}{dt} = \frac{v_L(0^+)}{L}$.


### 1. Key Parameters

*   **$\alpha$ (Neper Frequency / Damping Factor):** Represents how fast the circuit dissipates energy.
    
    *   **Series RLC:** $\alpha = \frac{R}{2L}$
        
    *   **Parallel RLC:** $\alpha = \frac{1}{2RC}$
        
*   **$\omega_0$ (Resonant Frequency):** The speed the circuit oscillates at if there were zero resistance.
    
    *   **Formula:** $\omega_0 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{LC}}$
        

* * *

### 2. The Characteristic Equation

The behavior is determined by the roots ($s_1, s_2$) of the quadratic equation:

$$s^2 + 2\alpha s + \omega_0^2 = 0$$

The roots are calculated as:

$$s_{1,2} = -\alpha \pm \sqrt{\alpha^2 - \omega_0^2}$$

* * *


| Case | Condition | Roots | Response Equation Form $x(t)$ |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Overdamped** | $\alpha > \omega_0$ | Real & Distinct ($s_1, s_2$) | $A_1 e^{s_1 t} + A_2 e^{s_2 t}$ |
| **Critically Damped** | $\alpha = \omega_0$ | Real & Repeated ($s_1 = s_2 = -\alpha$) | $(A_1 + A_2 t)e^{-\alpha t}$ |
| **Underdamped** | $\alpha < \omega_0$ | Complex Conjugates ($-\alpha \pm j\omega_d$) | $e^{-\alpha t}(B_1 \cos(\omega_d t) + B_2 \sin(\omega_d t))$ |
 -------

### 4. Important Definitions for Underdamped Case

*   **Damped Natural Frequency ($\omega_d$):** The actual frequency of oscillation when resistance is present.
    
    $$\omega_d = \sqrt{\omega_0^2 - \alpha^2}$$
    
*   **Damping Ratio ($\zeta$):** Sometimes used instead of $\alpha$ where  $$\zeta = \frac{\alpha}{\omega_0}$$
    
    *   $\zeta > 1$: Overdamped, $\zeta = 1$: Critically Damped, $\zeta < 1$: Underdamped
        

* * *

### 5. Quick Problem Solving Steps

1.  **Identify Circuit Type:** Determine if it is **Series** or **Parallel**.
    
2.  **Calculate Parameters:** Find **$\alpha$** and **$\omega_0$**.
    
3.  **Find the Forced Response ($x_\infty$):** Determine the "final state" of the circuit ($t \to \infty$). Capacitors become open circuits, inductors become short circuits.

        
4.  **Determine the damped**
    
5.  **Write the Complete Response:**  $x(t) = x_\infty + (\text{Natural Response Form from table})$
    
6.  **Solve for Constants:** Use initial conditions ($x(0)$ and $dx(0)/dt$) on the **entire** equation from Step 5 to find $A_1$ and $A_2$.

### **1. The Rules of "Deactivation"**
To isolate a source, replace all other **independent** sources as follows:

| Source Type | Replace With | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Voltage Source** | **Short Circuit** | A wire ($V = 0$) |
| **Current Source** | **Open Circuit** | A break ($I = 0$) |
| **Dependent Source** | **DO NOT TOUCH** | Must stay active! |



---

### **2. The Workflow**
1.  **Isolate:** Leave only **one** independent source "ON."
2.  **Analyze:** Find your target $v$ or $i$ for this specific state.
3.  **Iterate:** Repeat for every independent source in the circuit.
4.  **Sum:** Add all individual results algebraically ($V_{total} = v_1 + v_2 \dots$).

---

### **3. Critical Constraints**
* **Dependent Sources:** Since they rely on circuit variables (like $v_x$), they stay "ON" and react to whichever independent source is currently active.
* **Power ($P$):** **NEVER** use superposition for power ($P_{total} \neq P_1 + P_2$). Sum the voltages or currents first, then calculate $P = I^2 R$.
